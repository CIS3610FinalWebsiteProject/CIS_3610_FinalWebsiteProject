Why Visit Hokkaido?

Hokkaido offers a different experience compared to the rest of Japan.It is less crowded, more nature-focused, and rich in local culture. Visitors can enjoy beautiful scenery, seasonal activities, and unique food all in one place.

What is Hokkaido?

Hokkaido is the northernmost and second-largest of Japan’s four main islands, known for its cold climate, open landscapes, and unique history. Unlike the rest of Japan, it was developed later in the 1800s, giving it a distinct identity. It is also home to the Ainu people, the indigenous population of the region, whose traditions still influence Hokkaido today. Because of its colder weather and location, Hokkaido is less crowded and more local-focused compared to major cities like Tokyo or Osaka.

How to Get There

The easiest way to reach Hokkaido is by flying into New Chitose Airport, located near Sapporo. Domestic travelers can take flights, trains, or ferries from major cities across Japan. International should first fly into Tokyo (Narita or Haneda), then transfer to a direct flight to Hokkaido. Direct flights are also available from major Asian cities such as Seoul, Taipei, Shanghai, and Singapore.

What to Do

Hokkaido offers activities year-round for all types of travelers. In winter, visitors can enjoy skiing, snowboarding, and ice walking. The region is known worldwide for its powder snow. Outdoor activities include scenic hiking and fly-fishing, which is available year-round with minimal restrictions. In the cities, visitors can explore Sapporo, the capital of Hokkaido, and visit the Sapporo Beer Museum.

Food & Culture

Hokkaido is famous for its regional food, especially ramen. Sapporo is known for miso ramen, Asahikawa for soy sauce-based ramen, and Hakodate for salt-based ramen. Sushi lovers can visit small local sushi bars for a more authentic experience instead of commercial restaurants.
